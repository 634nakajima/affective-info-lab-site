About

Our research is organized around four pillars, explored through artistic practice

01

Multimodal / Crossmodal Affectivity

Expanding and transforming experience through sensory interactions. We explore what new affective experiences emerge when different sensory modalities intersect.

02

Enactive Affectivity

Exploration and emergence of sensory interest through action. We design spaces where the experiencer's active engagement triggers unexpected affective experiences.

03

Digital Kineticism

Constructing fields where information and physical objects co-constitute through movement. Digital information manifests as physical motion, and physical motion generates information.

04

Hapsonic Art

Exploring the significance of touch in sound art. We investigate how various tactile submodalities — thermal, kinesthetic, and more — transform the acoustic experience.

Practice-based Research

We explore our research themes through the practice of creating artworks. Our guiding principles: never make the experiencer a puppet, and emphasize the separation of everyday and extraordinary (liminoid formation).

Seminar Exercises

Becoming Aware of Kansei Values Around Us

A group workshop exploring concepts that emerge from combining everyday objects with various impressions (onomatopoeia, adjectives, sensory qualities).

  1. List as many impression words as possible and number them (15 min, goal: 50)
  2. Classify the impressions (20 min)
    • Visual, auditory, tactile, olfactory, gustatory
    • Other sensory impressions (e.g. restless, excited)
    • Other abstract impressions (e.g. luxurious, primitive, futuristic)
  3. List as many everyday objects as possible and number them (goal: 50)
  4. For each randomly combined impression + object pair, if it existed:
    • What shape would it be? (sketch if possible!)
    • What color? What texture? What sound?
    • Would it be useful (does it have value)?
  5. Share each group's combinations

Generating Ideas for Expressing Kansei Values

A group workshop brainstorming new expressions by combining everyday objects, places, and value-linked impressions.

  1. Classify listed impressions into 3 categories: Positive | Negative | Neutral/Context-dependent
  2. Randomly select one positive (or negative) impression, then list as many associated objects, places, etc. as possible
  3. Randomly select one from the list, then devise a way to express that impression without directly presenting the word or the actual thing
